The Book of Jasher

The Upright Record
Cited twice in the Hebrew Bible (Joshua 10:13, 2 Samuel 1:18) but excluded from the canon. Lost and refound multiple times over three millennia.

The Book of Jasher is cited by name in the Hebrew Bible itself — twice, in Joshua and 2 Samuel — yet it was never included in the canon. This means the compilers of the Old Testament were aware of a book they considered authoritative enough to quote but chose not to include. The reasons have never been satisfactorily explained.

The text we have fills in extraordinary gaps in the Biblical narrative: the backstory of Abraham and Nimrod, the childhood of Moses, events between Jacob and Esau that Genesis only hints at, and details of the Exodus that change the political and military picture entirely. Whether this specific manuscript is the original Book of Jasher or a medieval reconstruction remains disputed by scholars, but the text has been in circulation since at least the 9th century AD.

“And the sons of men in those days took from the cattle of the earth, the beasts of the field and the fowls of the air, and taught the mixture of animals of one species with the other.”

The Bible quotes it. The Bible excludes it. Draw your own conclusions.
