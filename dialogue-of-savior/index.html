The Dialogue of the Savior

Nag Hammadi Edition
Written mid-2nd century. Contains teachings of Jesus absent from all canonical gospels. Buried circa 390 AD, recovered at Nag Hammadi 1945.

The Dialogue of the Savior presents Jesus in private conversation with three disciples — Matthew, Judas (not Iscariot), and Mary Magdalene. The conversation ranges across questions of creation, death, the nature of light, and how the soul escapes the material world. Mary Magdalene speaks as an equal, and her understanding exceeds the male disciples. At one point, the text says she spoke as a woman who had understood completely.

The text is damaged — significant portions are missing, destroyed before the jar was sealed or lost to moisture over sixteen centuries. What remains suggests a Jesus who teaches continuous spiritual practice, not doctrine; inner transformation, not institutional membership. The canonical Church could accommodate none of this.

“Pray in the place where there is no woman. Destroy the works of womanhood.”

Parts of this were deliberately destroyed before it was buried. What remains is still extraordinary.
