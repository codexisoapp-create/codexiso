The Sibylline Oracles

Complete Edition
2nd century BC–5th century AD. Prophecies attributed to ancient Sibyls, used to legitimise both Jewish and Christian claims. The pagan originals were burned by the Roman Senate.

The Sibylline Oracles are a collection of prophetic verses attributed to the ancient Sibyls — Greek prophetesses who spoke for Apollo. The original Roman Sibylline Books were kept in a vault beneath the Temple of Jupiter and could only be consulted by special commission of the Roman Senate in times of crisis. In 83 BC, the vault burned. The Senate attempted to reconstruct the oracles but the result was judged inauthentic and eventually destroyed by the general Stilicho in 405 AD.

What survives are fourteen books of oracles assembled by Jewish and Christian writers between the 2nd century BC and the 5th century AD, who used the authoritative voice of the Sibyl to lend prophetic weight to their own theological agendas. The result is a fascinating palimpsest: layers of Jewish apocalypticism, Christian prophecy about Jesus, attacks on Rome, and genuine fragments of pagan oracular tradition.

“A great king shall come from Asia, a prince of a great host, who shall destroy the Roman power and lift up a holy banner over the earth.”

The Romans burned their own sacred prophecies. Jews and Christians rewrote them. Here they are.
