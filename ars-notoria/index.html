The Ars Notoria

The Notory Art of Solomon
13th century, claiming Solomonic origin. A system of prayers and magical diagrams for acquiring all human knowledge. Condemned by Thomas Aquinas as diabolical.

The Ars Notoria — the Notory Art — is a medieval magical text claiming to teach the acquisition of all knowledge through a combination of prayer, fasting, meditation on magical diagrams called “notae,” and the pronunciation of specific orations in Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and an unknown tongue. The system promises mastery of all seven liberal arts, perfect memory, eloquence, and ultimately a vision of God.

Thomas Aquinas condemned the Ars Notoria explicitly in his Summa Theologiae as diabolical magic that achieved its effects through dealings with demons rather than through legitimate intellectual effort. The irony is that the Ars Notoria is one of the most explicitly religious grimoires: it works through prayer, not compulsion, and its goal is enlightenment, not power. Aquinas’s condemnation ensured its widespread circulation — forbidden books were always the most sought after.

“By these prayers and orations, with the figures here presented, a man may obtain the knowledge of all Arts and Sciences, perfect Memory, Eloquence of Speech, and the Grace of God.”
