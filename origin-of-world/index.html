On the Origin of the World

Gnostic Cosmology
Written 3rd–4th century. Part of the Nag Hammadi collection, buried 390 AD, recovered 1945. A complete alternative cosmology.

On the Origin of the World is the most ambitious cosmological text in the Nag Hammadi library. It does not merely retell Genesis — it constructs an entire alternative universe, from the first stirring of darkness before creation through to the final apocalypse. It incorporates Egyptian, Greek, Jewish, and Christian traditions into a single sweeping mythology that refuses every orthodoxy.

The text describes Sophia (Wisdom) projecting a reflection into matter, creating the first darkness. From this comes the Demiurge and his Archons. Paradise is not God’s garden but the Archons’ prison. The serpent is the instructor. Phoenix and griffin walk alongside Adam and Eve. The divine spark in humanity predates and exceeds the creator god.

For institutional religion of any kind, this text is dangerous not because it is wrong but because it is too comprehensive to be easily refuted. It was buried. Here it is.

“Before chaos came into being, darkness existed, and after it shadow, and after shadow primary matter, and matter was within chaos.”

They could not refute it. So they buried it.
