The War Scroll

Sons of Light vs Sons of Darkness
1st century BC. The military manual of the apocalyptic war between the Children of Light and the Children of Darkness. Hidden at Qumran 68 AD, recovered 1947.

The War Scroll is the most extraordinary military manual in the ancient world. It describes in precise tactical detail a forty-year apocalyptic war between the Sons of Light (the Qumran community and their angelic allies) and the Sons of Darkness (the Romans, the Kittim, and the fallen angels led by Belial). The document specifies troop formations, banner inscriptions, trumpet signals, priestly prayers, and the precise role the archangel Michael plays at each stage of the battle.

The text reveals the militant messianism at the heart of the Dead Sea Scrolls community and provides crucial context for the Jewish revolts against Rome in 66–73 and 132–135 AD. It also illuminates the apocalyptic worldview shared by the Qumran sect, John the Baptist, and early Christianity. These connections — between armed Jewish apocalypticism and the origins of Christianity — were exactly what the Vatican scholars who controlled scroll access did not want published.

“This shall be a time of salvation for the people of God, an age of dominion for all the members of His company, and of everlasting destruction for the company of Belial.”
