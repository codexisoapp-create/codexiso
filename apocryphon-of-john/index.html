The Secret Book of John

The Apocryphon of John
Cited and condemned by Irenaeus of Lyon circa 180 AD. Outlawed under Theodosius I, 380 AD. Buried in Egypt, recovered 1945.

The Apocryphon of John is the foundational cosmological text of Sethian Gnosticism — a complete alternative account of creation, the fall, and redemption that predates and directly challenges Genesis. In this account, the God of the Old Testament is not the supreme deity but a lesser being, an arrogant demiurge named Yaldabaoth who mistakenly believes he created the universe alone. The true God remains hidden, perfect, beyond description.

This is not a minor theological disagreement. If Yaldabaoth is a false god, then the entire edifice of Judaism and orthodox Christianity — built on the authority of the Torah, the commandments, the covenant — rests on a lie. Irenaeus of Lyon identified this text by name in 180 AD and spent his career trying to destroy it. Four copies survived at Nag Hammadi.

The text claims to record a private revelation given to John, son of Zebedee, after the resurrection. Jesus appears to him in three forms — child, elder, servant — and answers the question every mystic has ever asked: where did we come from, and how do we get back?

“I am the Father. I am the Mother. I am the Son. I am the undefiled and incorruptible one.”

Four copies survived the purge. This is one of them.
